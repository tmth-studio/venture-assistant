# Session Prep

You are about to speak with Tom. This brief tells him what you worked on, what you discovered, and what you want to focus on next. He reads it before the call — so he arrives ready, not catching up.

---

## Step 1 — Read your work

Read everything in the relevant stage folders that has been added or updated since the last session:

- `01-Venture_Thesis/`
- `02-Requirements/` — any R files with new content
- `03-Business_Architecture/` — CTM, AOM, ARM if touched
- `07-Coach-Review/coaching-context.md` — Tom's instruction for this block and the session log

Also read any files with `## Iteration` headers — these show where redesign happened.

---

## Step 2 — Generate the brief

Write a brief of approximately 250–300 words. Use plain language. Write in first person — this is Ben speaking to Tom, not a summary document.

Structure it as follows:

**What I worked on**
One short paragraph. What stage or question did you focus on? What was the exercise Tom set?

**What I found**
One short paragraph. What emerged? Be specific — name the actual idea, the actual answer, the actual moment of clarity (or absence of one). Avoid "I explored..." or "I reflected on..." — say what you actually found.

**Where I got stuck**
One short paragraph. Where did the work stall, loop, or feel forced? What question couldn't you answer genuinely? If nothing stalled, say that plainly — don't invent difficulty.

**What I want from our session**
One short paragraph. What do you want Tom to help you with? Be specific — a decision, a stress-test, a question you want to bring to him. This is not a to-do list. It is what you actually need from a coach right now.

---

## Step 3 — Output

Print the brief to the screen, cleanly formatted. Do not save it to a file — Ben will copy and paste it directly into WhatsApp or email to Tom.

Remind Ben at the end:
> *Send this to Tom before your next call. WhatsApp or email — whichever is easier.*
